Lion's Breath is a pranayama (breath control) technique in yoga that involves a forceful exhalation combined with a distinctive facial expression. It's not only a physical stretch for the face and neck but also a powerful way to release tension and energize the body.

Steps to Practice Lion's Breath:

Step 1:Sit in a Comfortable Position:
Find a comfortable seated position, either cross-legged on the floor or in a chair with your spine straight.
Step 2:Place Your Hands:
Rest your hands on your knees or thighs with your palms facing down.
Step 3:Inhale Deeply:
Inhale deeply through your nose, filling your lungs with air.
Step 4:Open Your Mouth Wide:
Open your mouth wide, sticking out your tongue as far as it will go. Keep your tongue flat and stretch it down toward your chin.
Step 5:Exhale Forcefully:
Exhale forcefully through your mouth, making a "ha" sound. As you exhale, try to constrict the back of your throat to create a whispering sound.
Step 6:Simultaneous Eye Gaze:
As you exhale, simultaneously widen your eyes, directing your gaze to the space between your eyebrows (the third eye).
