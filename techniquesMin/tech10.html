Deep Breathing

This yoga breathing practice’s unique sensation helps create instant calm and is especially soothing around your forehead.

Some people use humming bee breath to relieve frustration, anxiety, and anger. Research shows it may help reduceTrusted Source your heart rate, think more clearly, and feel less irritable or stressed.

Of course, you’ll want to practice it in a place where you’re free to make a humming sound.

Steps for Deep Breathing

Step 1:Find a Comfortable Seat:
Sit in a comfortable position, either on the floor with crossed legs or in a chair with your feet flat on the ground. Ensure your spine is straight, and your shoulders are relaxed.
Step 2:Relax Your Body:
Close your eyes if it feels comfortable, and take a few moments to let go of tension in your muscles. Relax your facial muscles, shoulders, and any areas where you may be holding onto stress.
Step 3:Place Your Hands:
SRest your index fingers on the cartilage between your cheek and ear, and let the other fingers lightly rest on your head. Alternatively, you can place your hands on your face with your thumbs covering your ears, allowing your fingers to gently rest on the back of your head.
Step 4:Inhale Deeply:
Inhale deeply and slowly through your nose, filling your lungs with air. Feel your chest and abdomen expand as you breathe in.
Step 5:Exhale with a Humming Sound:
Close your lips and teeth gently, creating a slight resistance. As you exhale, produce a low-pitched humming sound like that of a bee. Allow the sound to be steady and continuous throughout the exhalation.
Step 6:Feel the Vibration:
As you hum, focus on feeling the gentle vibration created by the sound. Let the vibration resonate within your head and chest.
Step 7: Inhale and Repeat:
After completing the humming exhalation, inhale deeply again through your nose, and then exhale with the humming sound. Repeat this process for several rounds.
