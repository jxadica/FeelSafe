Deep Breathing

Deep breathing helps to relieve shortness of breath by preventing air from getting trapped in your lungs and helping you to breathe in fresher air. It may help you to feel more relaxed and centered.

Steps for Deep Breathing

Step 1:Find a Comfortable Seat:
Sit in a comfortable position, either on the floor with crossed legs or in a chair with your feet flat on the ground. Ensure your spine is straight, and your shoulders are relaxed.
Step 2:Relax Your Body:
Close your eyes if it feels comfortable, and take a few moments to let go of tension in your muscles. Relax your facial muscles, shoulders, and any areas where you may be holding onto stress.
Step 3:Focus on Your Breath:
Shift your attention to your breath. Begin by taking a few natural breaths, noticing the sensation of air entering and leaving your body.
Step 4:Inhale Deeply Through Your Nose:
Inhale slowly and deeply through your nose. Allow your abdomen to expand fully, feeling the breath fill the lower part of your lungs. Your chest should rise only slightly.
Step 5:Exhale Completely Through Your Mouth:
Exhale slowly and completely through your mouth. Feel your abdomen naturally contract as you release the breath. Focus on the sensation of letting go.
Step 6:Count Your Breath:
As you continue deep breathing, count the duration of your inhalation and exhalation. Try to make each breath smooth and controlled. For example, inhale for a count of four and exhale for a count of six.
Step 7: Engage Your Diaphragm:
Place more emphasis on the diaphragmatic breath by allowing your abdomen to rise and fall with each inhalation and exhalation. This ensures you are utilizing the full capacity of your lungs.
