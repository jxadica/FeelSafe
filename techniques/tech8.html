Sitali breath

Welcome to the refreshing practice of Sitali Breath, a cooling and calming pranayama technique that draws inspiration from ancient yogic traditions. Also known as the "cooling breath" or "serpent breath," Sitali Breath involves inhaling air through a rolled tongue, creating a unique and soothing sensation. This practice is not only a physical breath exercise but also a mental and energetic one, offering a sense of relaxation and tranquility. Join us as we explore the steps to unlock the cooling power of Sitali Breath.

Steps for Sitali Breath:

Step 1:Find a Comfortable Seat:
Sit in a comfortable position, either on the floor with crossed legs or in a chair with your feet flat on the ground. Ensure your spine is straight, and your shoulders are relaxed.
Step 2:Relax and Center:
Close your eyes if you feel comfortable doing so. Take a few natural breaths to settle into the present moment, letting go of any tension in your body.
Step 3:Roll Your Tongue:
Extend your tongue and roll it into a tube, creating a straw-like shape. If you are unable to roll your tongue, you can purse your lips, creating a small "O" shape.
Step 4:Inhale Through the Rolled Tongue:
Inhale slowly and deeply through the rolled tongue, allowing the air to pass over the surface of the tongue. Feel the coolness of the breath as it enters your mouth.
Step 5:Close Your Mouth:
After inhaling fully, close your mouth. If you rolled your tongue, gently rest the tip against the roof of your mouth. If you pursed your lips, close them gently.
Step 6:Exhale Through Your Nose:
Exhale slowly and completely through your nose. Focus on a controlled and steady breath, allowing the warm air to pass through your nasal passages.
Step 7: Repeat the Cycle:
Continue the cycle by inhaling through the rolled tongue, closing your mouth, and exhaling through your nose. Maintain a smooth and comfortable rhythm.
