Alternate nostril breathing

One powerful technique that has transcended time and cultures is Alternate Nostril Breathing, also known as Nadi Shodhana or Anulom Vilom. Rooted in the ancient practice of pranayama from yoga traditions, this breathing exercise offers a simple yet profound way to harmonize the mind, body, and spirit. Whether you're seeking stress relief, enhanced focus, or a moment of tranquility, alternate nostril breathing can be your guide to a balanced and centered existence.

Steps for Alternate Nostril Breathing:

Step 1:Sit in a Comfortable Position:
Find a comfortable seated position, either cross-legged on the floor or in a chair with your spine straight.
Step 2:Nasal Mudra:
Use your right thumb to close off your right nostril and your ring finger or pinky finger to close off your left nostril. The other fingers can rest gently on your forehead or in a comfortable position.
Step 3:Inhale through the Left Nostril:
Close off your right nostril and inhale deeply and slowly through your left nostril. Fill your lungs to a comfortable capacity.
Step 4:Pause and Switch:
Close both nostrils briefly, either holding your breath or inhaling and exhaling lightly through both nostrils. This marks the transition between inhalation and exhalation.
Step 5:Exhale through the Right Nostril:
Close your left nostril and exhale slowly and completely through your right nostril. Empty your lungs comfortably.
Step 6:Inhale through the Right Nostril:
Keeping your left nostril closed, inhale deeply and slowly through your right nostril. Fill your lungs with air.
Step 7: Pause and Switch:
Close both nostrils briefly, holding the breath or breathing lightly through both nostrils, marking the transition between exhalation and inhalation.
Step 8: Exhale through the Left Nostril:
Close your right nostril and exhale slowly and completely through your left nostril. Empty your lungs comfortably.
Step 9: Repeat the Cycle:
Continue this alternating pattern, inhaling through one nostril, pausing, and exhaling through the other nostril. Aim for a smooth and controlled breath, maintaining a sense of calm throughout the practice.
